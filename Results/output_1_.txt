Acute Myocardial Infarction (STEMI) – ECG shows clear ST-elevation in anterior leads (V1-V4) combined with crushing chest pain and diaphoresis, which is diagnostic for a heart attack.

Hypertensive Urgency – Blood pressure of 160/100 mmHg indicates significant hypertension that is exacerbating cardiac strain.

Coronary Artery Disease – Long-term history of smoking (30 years) and high cholesterol are strong risk factors suggesting underlying arterial blockage.
